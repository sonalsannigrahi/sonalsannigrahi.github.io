About

I am a final year student following a Mathematics and Computer Science double major at École Polytechnique within the Bachelor Programme. Having started coding only in 2018 as part of my introductory Python programming course, I would've never guessed how much I would have come around to loving computer science! With my background in mathematics, I quickly began to appreciate the problem solving component of Computer Science. However, something was still missing for me.

In came my passion for languages! Combining linguistics, mathematics, and computer science seemed like an impossible task before I knew of the wonders of NLP. Growing up in India, I developed a quadrilingual background due to which I recognise the challenges that translation poses and more so the challenges it poses for complex languages such as those found in India. I am determined to make translation systems better for low resource languages and also improve current language modelling tools for South Asian languages.

Research



My current research interests lie at the intersection of Computer Vision and Natural Language Processing. I hope to focus within the areas of multi-modal translations, with attention given to low resource languages. I am working on, and have worked on, some exciting projects! Here are my major contributions in fields ranging from biocomputing to my current interests in language technology.

  • Fall 2020 Under the advisory of Jesse Read at the DaSciM team at LIX, I am completing my Bachelor's thesis on Context Aware Cross Lingual Representations for Low Resource Machine Translation.
  • Fall 2020 Under the advisory of Vicky Kalogeiton at the GeoViC team at LIX, I am conduting a research project in Self-supervised learning of Human interactions through synchronising human tracks. Read my report here!
  • Summer 2020 Under the advisory of Zied Sellami at Linagora, I built a bilingual next word prediction model for English and French. I studied different Word2Vec architecutres and experimented with transfer learning via BERT and Open GPT language models. Furthermore, I was tasked with building industrial level solutions which led me to study model compression via knowledge distillation. I wrote about my findings in the company's medium blog!
  • Spring 2020 Under the advisory of Thomas Simonson at BioC, I developed a Monte-Carlo method to determine characteristic sequence alignments for Tyrosine Kinase proteins. Read my report here!
  • Summer 2019 Under the advisory of Pierre Boyer at CREST, I studied impacts of large scale social media movments (on Twitter) on local legislation. I built tools to track real time tweets and get geolocation data which I then extrapolated into chloropleth maps. Finally, I performed sentiment analysis on bilingual tweets using a Naïve Bayes classifier.
